About Digital ASIC Tapeout

The Digital ASIC Tapeout project, sponsored by Northrop Grumman, focuses on training a team of students to proficiently execute the full-flow digital design of an Application-Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC). This hands-on project challenges students to implement a RISC-V processor core using industry-standard Electronic Design Automation (EDA) tools such as Cadence, while adhering to advanced design principles.

The project’s main objective is to develop a custom digital ASIC capable of efficiently processing 100 million instructions per second (100 MIPS), while operating within strict power and thermal constraints. The design will be optimized for 45nm or smaller technology nodes, showcasing the team’s ability to tackle modern semiconductor challenges.

Project Goals

  • Design a RISC-V Core: The core will implement the RISC-V RV32I base instruction set, targeting high performance and low power consumption.
  • Verification and Testing: The design will undergo rigorous functional and formal verification using the Universal Verification Methodology (UVM), with a target of at least 85% code coverage.
  • Physical Design: The design will be synthesized and laid out using cutting-edge tools to ensure manufacturability, adhering to design rule checks (DRC) and layout vs schematic (LVS) validation.

Key Challenges

  • Power Efficiency: The design is expected to operate within a voltage range of 0.95-1.25V, while consuming less than 1W of power.
  • High Performance: Achieving a processing speed of 100 MIPS with integrated L1 cache.
  • Manufacturability: Ensure the design meets stringent industry standards for timing, IR drop, and electromigration, critical for ASIC manufacturing.

This project is a cornerstone of Northrop Grumman’s initiative to build a pipeline of future ASIC engineers proficient in the tools and techniques necessary to bring custom chip designs from concept to tapeout.
